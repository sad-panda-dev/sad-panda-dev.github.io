I had spent so many hours studying, reading, researching, and learning about any aspect of cooking or what it takes to manage and run a professional kitchen. KEEP GOING

I have always been interested in computers and the capabilities that they have. So I thought that perhaps a career in computer science would be something that I find interesting. I then started to do a deep dive into what field of computer science I would like to pursue. I was almost immediately drawn to software development. The idea that there is an underlying set of rules as to how you can and should create software, but there is still room for creativity with regards to how the developer goes about solving the problem, seemed very similar to the aspects of cooking that first drew me to it. I started by learning more about the python programming language, and began to develop very basic rudimentary programs. But I was hooked, and it wasn't long after that I made the decision to go back to school and pursue a degree in computer science. The more I learn about the demands of the job of software development, the more I realize that my years of experience in professional kitchens are going to be very useful. Timing is everything, and while it is important to work well as an individual at times, it is imperative to be able to be a productive team member. I was constantly learning new techniques for different dishes I wished to create or recreate, software development will require that same tenacious nature of always looking for the newest advancements and learning how to implement them. It will allow me to keep my work as a source of my creativity, and therefore allow me to be more passionate about what I do for work.
